# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming, inclusive, and respectful environment for everyone involved in this project.

We expect all contributors, maintainers, and users of `zero-copy-env` to adhere to this Code of Conduct in all project spaces, including issues, pull requests, discussions, and related communication channels.

---

## Expected Behavior

All participants are expected to:

- Be respectful and considerate in communication
- Focus on constructive feedback and technical discussion
- Respect differing opinions and approaches
- Accept constructive criticism gracefully
- Prioritize collaboration over conflict
- Assume good intent from others

---

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are considered unacceptable:

- Harassment, discrimination, or personal attacks of any kind
- Offensive, derogatory, or inflammatory comments
- Trolling or disruptive behavior
- Publishing private information without consent
- Repeated unsolicited negative behavior
- Any conduct that would be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces, including:

- GitHub issues and pull requests
- Code reviews
- Discussions
- Any communication related to the project

It also applies when representing the project in external communities.

---

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for:

- Reviewing reported incidents
- Investigating violations fairly and consistently
- Taking appropriate corrective actions when necessary

---

## Enforcement Guidelines

Maintainers may take the following actions in response to violations:

### 1. Warning
A private warning explaining the inappropriate behavior and requesting correction.

### 2. Temporary ban
Temporary restriction from participation in project spaces.

### 3. Permanent ban
Permanent removal from participation in severe or repeated cases.

---

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it by opening a private issue or contacting the maintainers directly.

All reports will be handled confidentially.
